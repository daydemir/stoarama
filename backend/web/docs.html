Overview

Browse the catalog, record on a schedule.

Stoarama is two things: a public catalog of live video streams you can browse without an account, and a paid recorder that captures a stream on a schedule and saves each capture as a clip you can download. Browsing is free and open. Recording needs a signed-in account and a card on file.

Questions? Email deniz@aydemir.us.

How it works

From browse to clips

  • Browse the catalog on /streams. No account needed.
  • Open a stream to see its metadata, a live preview where the source allows it, and a self-record command.
  • Sign in (emailed link, no password) and set up a recording on /recordings.
  • Watch, download, or delete your clips, and see your bill on /account.
What recording costs

Pay for what you record

  • $0.50 for each day a recording captures at least one clip. Days with nothing recorded are free.
  • $0.10 per GB-month for clips you keep in Stoarama-managed storage. A clip kept a few days costs a small fraction of that.
  • Billed once a month in arrears with a card on file via Stripe. Adding a card does not charge you.
  • Bring your own S3 bucket instead and Stoarama charges only the record-day fee.
Recording

Record any public HLS or HTTPS stream.

A recording captures one stream on a schedule and writes each capture as a clip. Set it up on /recordings. You point it at a stream, pick how often it fires and how long each clip is, and choose where the clips land.

Sources

What can be recorded

  • A public HLS stream (a .m3u8 URL).
  • A direct HTTP(S) video stream.
  • YouTube is view-only and cannot be hosted-recorded. To capture a YouTube stream, use the yt-dlp command on its detail page from your own machine.
Schedule & clip length

How often, how long

  • Presets: every 15 min, every 30 min, hourly, every 4 hours, daily at a time in your timezone, or a custom 5-field cron.
  • Fires are at least 10 minutes apart.
  • Clip length: 1, 5, or 15 minutes, or a custom 5 to 900 seconds. It must be shorter than the gap between fires.
Storage

Managed or your own bucket

  • Stoarama-managed storage when available, billed at $0.10 per GB-month for the time each clip is kept.
  • Your own S3-compatible bucket: Cloudflare R2, Amazon S3, Backblaze B2, Wasabi, DigitalOcean Spaces, or any S3-compatible endpoint. Credentials are connection-checked before a recording can use them.
Managing recordings

Pause, resume, delete

  • The /recordings list shows each recording's status and health, a live cost ledger, and a per-recording monthly estimate.
  • Pause or resume a recording at any time, or delete it.
Clips

Working with captured clips

  • Each recording has its own clip page with a paginated list. View a clip inline or download it.
  • Download all clips on the page as a ZIP with an in-zip manifest.csv (id, filename, start, end, duration_ms, size_bytes, object_key, status).
  • Send any clip to another of your S3 buckets. The original is kept.
  • Delete a single clip or all clips for a recording.
Catalog & self-record

Browse the catalog, or capture it yourself.

The catalog on /streams is open to everyone. Each stream has a detail page with its metadata, a live preview where the source allows it, a Record this stream button that opens the recorder pre-filled, and a copy-paste command to capture it from your own machine.

Browsing

Catalog segments

  • Recordable: HLS and direct-video streams the hosted recorder accepts.
  • Still image: snapshot sources that refresh on a poll.
  • YouTube: view-only streams you can watch and self-record.
  • Korea: streams from Korean public sources, on /korea.
Record this yourself

Capture on your own machine

Each detail page gives a command tailored to the stream type. For an HLS or direct-video stream:

ffmpeg -i "<resolved .m3u8 or HTTPS URL>" -t 60 -c copy "stream.mp4"

For a YouTube stream:

yt-dlp "<watch URL>"

Both need the named tool installed. -t 60 records 60 seconds; remove it to record until you stop.
